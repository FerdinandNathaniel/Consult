## Summary of the Week

The week revealed a fundamental disconnect between AI industry valuations and demonstrated business model sustainability. [Anthropic raised $65 billion at a near-trillion-dollar valuation](https://the-decoder.com/claude-company-anthropic-nears-a-trillion-dollar-valuation-after-raising-65-billion-in-series-h/) despite uncertain long-term profitability, while [major corporations—including Microsoft and Uber—dramatically cut AI spending after discovering minimal productivity gains](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2060194655292309696), with [H200 rental prices collapsing from $7/hour to $4/hour](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2060194655292309696). ## What Were Followed Accounts Discussing This Week?

**AI Valuation Bubble and Market Sustainability Concerns** dominated the week's discourse. [Gary Marcus and industry analysts repeatedly warned of an unsustainable AI investment bubble driven by trillions in data center spending funded through pension funds and retail savings](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2058731956326854674), with [Google CEO Sundar Pichai admitting "irrationality in the current AI boom"](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2058705488666316974). **AI Implementation Reality vs. Hype** emerged as a critical theme. [ChatGPT's diagnosis of 40 million people with a completely fictional disease ("Bixonimania") demonstrated critical vulnerabilities in distinguishing fact from fabricated online information](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2060543247437668574).

**Open vs. Closed AI Models and Knowledge Equity** surfaced tensions around AI power concentration.
