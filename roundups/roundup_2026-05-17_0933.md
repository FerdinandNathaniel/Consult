# Weekly Round-Up — Week 19, 2026
*Based on 7 daily briefings · 11 May to 17 May 2026*

## Summary of the Week

The Dutch AI startup ecosystem demonstrated resilience through two significant funding rounds—[eyeo's €40 million Series A](https://tech.eu/2026/05/11/eyeo-raises-eur40m-to-improve-imaging-and-sensor-performance/) in nanophotonic imaging and [Ditto's €7.6 million Series funding](https://tech.eu/2026/05/12/dutch-healthtech-ditto-raises-eur76m-to-bring-ai-powered-patient-support-across-europe/)—signalling sustained institutional confidence in Dutch deep-tech and AI-enabled healthcare solutions despite broader market volatility. Concurrently, the [EU AI Act's Article 50 transparency requirements established an August 2026 compliance deadline](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/transparency-rules-article-50/?utm_source=rss&utm_medium=rss&utm_campaign=transparency-rules-article-50), creating immediate operational demands for Dutch and European AI companies seeking to scale within the EU market. The week also surfaced critical tensions between AI capability claims and demonstrated limitations: [experts highlighted that LLMs lack world models necessary for reliable agentic systems](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2054567753206419768), while [research showed executives report minimal AI impact on employment or productivity over three years](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2054010616495710267), contrasting sharply with venture capital narratives. Geopolitically, [China's AI infrastructure investment and chip supply improvements](https://the-decoder.com/tencent-plans-to-ramp-up-ai-spending-as-chinas-chip-supply-allegedly-improves/) and [efficiency advantages in model training](https://www.exponentialview.co/p/inside-chinese-ai-labs-efficiency-moat) underscore Europe's competitive positioning challenge relative to both the United States and China.

## What Were Followed Accounts Discussing This Week?

Expert discourse across the week coalesced around three recurring themes with direct policy and investment implications:

**AI Capability Limitations and Market Hype Correction**: [Gary Marcus](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2054015225318482075) challenged narratives of incremental LLM progress, arguing that recent advances like Claude Code vindicate neurosymbolic approaches rather than pure language models, while [noting the lack of transformative leaps since GPT-4](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2053999404076654800). [Marcus further documented that Anthropic's Mythos model has been overhyped relative to actual performance improvements](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2053992175164379596), and [highlighted NBER research showing executives report minimal AI impact on employment or productivity over three years](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2054010616495710267)—a critical data point for assessing whether current venture capital valuations reflect sustainable business models. [Additionally, Marcus warned that the AI circular funding bubble has grown to twice the size of 2008 subprime mortgage debt, with 40% of US retirement funds exposed to AI stocks](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2053546657485299869), and [noted that hyperscalers plan $755 billion capex in 2026 (+83% vs. 2025), consuming 100% of operating cash flows and forcing 64% cuts to buybacks](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2053689822074867899).

**AI Adoption Gap and Scaling Challenges**: [UBS data revealed that while surveys predicted 43% of companies would deploy AI "at scale," actual adoption stands at just 19%](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2054411403830632547), with industry insiders acknowledging that [scaling returns are diminishing](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2054411403830632547). [ByteDance reportedly shut down 30% of AI projects](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2054291742195691987), signalling that even major players are recalibrating investment strategies amid execution challenges.

**Corporate Governance and Transparency Concerns**: [Sam Altman confirmed indirect equity stakes in OpenAI that he failed to disclose during U.S. Senate testimony](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2054315174572466403), while [seven OpenAI insiders accused Altman of dishonesty](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2054409409485226363). These governance issues carry direct implications for investor confidence and regulatory scrutiny in the AI sector, particularly as [OpenAI prepares for an IPO](https://the-decoder.com/sam-altmans-personal-investments-face-political-scrutiny-ahead-of-openais-planned-ipo/).

**AI Labor Market Impact and Policy Framing**: [Andrew Ng argued there will be "no AI jobpocalypse," cautioning against "overblown stories of large-scale unemployment,"](https://x.com/AndrewYNg/status/2054236506756370865) while [Gary Marcus countered that claiming there will never be significant job displacement "hardly seems plausible,"](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2054364773438104044) with emerging consensus that AI will affect labor markets substantially but unevenly. [David Timis framed AI policy as fundamentally a labor policy question](https://x.com/DavidTimis/status/2055668943956713871) and [argued that datacenter expansion debates are ultimately about democratic governance](https://x.com/DavidTimis/status/2055699940848939326), emphasizing that without legitimate policy channels addressing AI's inequality-accelerating effects, public backlash becomes inevitable.

**AI Safety, Governance, and Regulatory Scrutiny**: [Gary Marcus amplified warnings about LLMs' potential for mass manipulation](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2053997204013244690) and [highlighted transparency issues at OpenAI regarding board audit committees and undisclosed conflict-of-interest reviews](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2053994338783469865). [Research showed that models can be fine-tuned to believe false claims, raising concerns about reliability and the potential for "poisoning science" through hallucinated references](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2055324956154634459). [Additionally, research demonstrated that AI models trained on web data inadvertently amplify state-controlled narratives, raising concerns about AI laundering authoritarian messaging](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2054775941491036278).

**Geopolitical AI Competition and Sovereignty**: [Yann LeCun promoted Project Tapestry, a collaborative initiative to build sovereign AI capabilities for Vietnam, Japan, India, and other nations](https://x.com/ylecun/status/2053524170739212479), positioning open-source development as a path to technological independence. [LeCun countered narratives of Silicon Valley dominance, noting major AI breakthroughs originated in Paris, Montreal, London, and Hangzhou](https://x.com/ylecun/status/2053524102573420693). [Thierry Breton framed AI competition in geopolitical terms, warning that Europe faces pressure from both the U.S. and China and must better defend its interests](https://x.com/ThierryBreton/status/2054602194796650841).

**Research Funding and Brain Drain Concerns**: [Yann LeCun highlighted severe cuts to US research funding, with NIH grants at historic lows and MIT reducing graduate students by 20%](https://x.com/ylecun/status/2055702985578025041), threatening American scientific competitiveness and suggesting potential talent migration relevant to European startup ecosystem positioning.
