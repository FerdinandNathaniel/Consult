# Weekly Round-Up — Week 23, 2026
*Based on 7 daily briefings · 1 June to 7 June 2026*

## Summary of the Week

The EU faces a critical competitive gap in AI infrastructure investment and intangible capital formation that directly constrains the startup investment climate. [ECB research demonstrates that the EU's productivity gap with the US is substantially attributable to underinvestment in intangible and tangible ICT capital](https://www.ecb.europa.eu//pub/pdf/scpwps/ecb.wp3242~873057c617.en.pdf), with particular shortfalls in communications equipment, R&D, and intellectual property development—a structural constraint that extends beyond aggregate spending to the quality and type of capital formation. Simultaneously, [the OECD released its AI Policy Toolkit to help governments translate AI principles into actionable frameworks](https://wp.oecd.ai/the-oecd-ai-policy-toolkit-better-ai-policies-for-better-lives/), offering Dutch policymakers practical mechanisms to balance innovation incentives with responsible governance. However, US-China-Europe competitive dynamics are intensifying: [SoftBank announced a €75 billion AI data center buildout in France](https://the-decoder.com/softbank-plans-75-billion-euro-ai-data-center-buildout-in-france/), while [Anthropic filed for IPO with a near-$1 trillion valuation](https://the-decoder.com/claude-maker-anthropic-files-for-ipo-with-the-sec/) and [SpaceX signed a $920 million-per-month GPU capacity deal with Google](https://the-decoder.com/spacex-signs-920-million-per-month-deal-with-google-for-110000-nvidia-ai-chips-ahead-of-ipo/), signaling unprecedented concentration of AI capital in the US. Expert observers flagged mounting concerns about AI market sustainability, with [Gary Marcus and David Timis warning of unsustainable valuations, circular financing schemes, and fundamental profitability challenges](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2062038461234946418) that could trigger systemic financial risk if major AI firms stumble.

## What Were Followed Accounts Discussing This Week?

### AI Market Valuation and Financial Stability

Expert debate intensified around whether the AI sector faces a bubble or represents genuine supply-constrained growth. [David Timis and industry observers argued the current cycle is supply-constrained rather than demand-constrained and therefore insulated from dot-com-style collapse](https://x.com/DavidTimis/status/2061175174519533993), but [Gary Marcus countered that escalating AI investment bets could trigger a "too big to fail" scenario with massive economic consequences if the sector stumbles](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2061083763862606235). By mid-week, concerns sharpened: [Gary Marcus and David Timis flagged unprecedented concentration risk in AI-related IPOs and semiconductor valuations, with three companies (SpaceX, OpenAI, Anthropic) projected to reach ~$3.75T in combined valuations—exceeding all 2,600 dot-com IPOs combined](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2061505704503206107), while [semiconductor stocks now represent 29% of the S&P 500, surpassing the 2000 dot-com bubble peak](https://x.com/DavidTimis/status/2061568184788906114). By week's end, [Gary Marcus documented signs of fundamental weakness, noting that OpenAI's reported pursuit of government investment signals "no path to profitability," while stock market declines across semiconductor and AI compute sectors suggest the market may be pricing in unsustainable business models](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2062998693364764788).

### AI Business Model Sustainability and Revenue Challenges

[Gary Marcus and Vivek Wadhwa discussed the collapse of AI revenue models, particularly the shift away from unlimited "all-you-can-eat" pricing toward pay-per-usage models](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2062333210034339878), with [Marcus arguing hyperscalers were forced to abandon unlimited pricing because they "literally could not afford" the hemorrhaging costs](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2062369417372258652). [David Timis framed the pricing debate as fundamentally about whether customers can connect spending to measurable value, not mere affordability](https://x.com/DavidTimis/status/2062243060860305585). By late week, [Gary Marcus highlighted that breakthroughs in AI model compression (e.g., Google's TurboVec reducing memory requirements by 16x) could undermine the economic case for massive GPU cluster and data center investments](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2063390324228305378).

### AI Technical Limitations and Strategic Research Directions

[Gary Marcus argued that large-scale pretrained models, while valuable, are insufficient for general AI and that focusing narrowly on near-term solvable challenges may not advance the field strategically](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2061295122655191378), reflecting ongoing expert skepticism about whether current large language model approaches can deliver on broader AI ambitions. [Marcus later predicted that neurosymbolic AI systems—not large language models—will become the profitable future, while LLMs will remain unprofitable except for chip manufacturers](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2062409773866455385).

### AI Hype and Capability Claims

[Gary Marcus and colleagues critiqued what they call the "AI bait and switch"—where companies shift goalposts on AI capabilities, conflating narrow task improvements (like faster coding) with genuine artificial general intelligence (AGI)](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2062657865031315550), with [Marcus arguing Anthropic's recent claims about recursive self-improvement are overstated and don't represent the breakthrough they suggest](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2062689785274364027). [Marcus also pushed back against both extreme positions on employment impact, predicting less than 5-10% workforce displacement in the near term and cautioning that AI industry leaders have repeatedly overstated employment impacts](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2062401202923667886).

### AI Ownership, IP Rights, and Wealth Distribution

[Gary Marcus and colleagues engaged with Bernie Sanders' proposal for public ownership stakes in AI, acknowledging both the legitimacy of concerns about intellectual property theft in AI training and the problematic concentration of AI wealth among a small number of billionaires](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2061532601492652494). [Marcus endorsed Andrew Yang's proposal to tax AI systems, emphasizing that generative AI leverages intellectual property from many creators who were not adequately compensated](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2062584645255057782).

### AI Market Commoditization and Competitive Dynamics

[Gary Marcus argued that despite industry assumptions of "winner-take-all" competition, AI lacks sustainable competitive moats because companies are building similar technical solutions with similar datasets](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/2061617157897912370), likely resulting in commodity pricing, price wars, and inability to sustain monopoly returns.

### AI Job Creation and Workforce Adaptation

## Most Relevant Items

### AI Infrastructure and Capital Allocation

**EU Productivity Gap Linked to Intangible ICT Capital Underinvestment**
The ECB's analysis of investment composition from 1996–2021 demonstrates that while overall investment levels in the EU and US are broadly similar, the EU invests significantly less in intangible and tangible ICT capital—particularly in communications equipment, R&D, and intellectual property development. This composition deficit is associated with a substantial portion of the EU's productivity gap with the US, directly constraining the startup investment climate and AI ecosystem competitiveness. This finding underscores that Dutch AI policy effectiveness will depend not merely on regulatory clarity but on whether policy settings actively mobilise intangible investment and ICT capital formation.
*Briefings: Wed 3 Jun, Thu 4 Jun*
[ECB Working Papers](https://www.ecb.europa.eu//pub/pdf/scpwps/ecb.wp3242~873057c617.en.pdf)

**SoftBank Plans €75 Billion AI Data Center Buildout in France**
SoftBank announced plans to build AI data centers with up to 5 gigawatts of capacity in France, the company's largest AI infrastructure investment in Europe, at up to €75 billion. By 2031, facilities worth €45 billion are set to go up at three sites in northern France. This mega-investment signals major capital flows into European AI infrastructure but raises questions about whether the Netherlands and other EU member states can compete for comparable infrastructure investment and whether European startups will have cost-competitive access to compute.
*Briefing: Mon 1 Jun*
[The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/softbank-plans-75-billion-euro-ai-data-center-buildout-in-france/)

**SpaceX Signs $920 Million Per Month GPU Capacity Deal with Google Ahead of IPO**
SpaceX is leasing AI computing capacity to Google for $920 million per month, giving Google access to approximately 110,000 Nvidia chips to meet demand for its Gemini Enterprise platform. The deal demonstrates acute scarcity of AI infrastructure globally and reveals how tightly big tech companies' businesses are now intertwined—a dynamic that constrains European startups' access to compute at competitive prices and highlights the concentration of AI infrastructure investment in the US.

[The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/spacex-signs-920-million-per-month-deal-with-google-for-110000-nvidia-ai-chips-ahead-of-ipo/)

### AI Policy and Governance Frameworks

**OECD AI Policy Toolkit Released to Guide Government Implementation**
The OECD released its comprehensive AI Policy Toolkit designed to translate high-level AI principles into actionable governance frameworks, addressing a critical gap between policy aspirations and implementation. The toolkit provides governments with practical guidance, policy examples, and global comparative insights, enabling jurisdictions like the Netherlands to benchmark their AI regulatory approaches against international standards while maintaining competitive advantage in attracting AI-driven startups and venture capital. The timing is significant for Dutch policymakers, as the toolkit offers a structured methodology for balancing innovation incentives with responsible AI governance.
*Briefings: Wed 3 Jun, Thu 4 Jun*
[OECD AI Policy Observatory](https://wp.oecd.ai/the-oecd-ai-policy-toolkit-better-ai-policies-for-better-lives/)

**EU Tech Sovereignty Package Aims to Reduce Reliance on US and Overseas Providers**
The European Commission unveiled its plan to reduce European reliance on US and overseas tech providers, with proposals to support research and innovation in cloud and AI technology, and to triple the capacity of European data centers within seven years. The package directly addresses European strategic autonomy and industrial policy but raises questions about whether Dutch and European startups will benefit from these initiatives or whether they will primarily advantage incumbent players and state-backed infrastructure projects.
*Briefing: Thu 4 Jun*
[Tech.eu](https://tech.eu/2026/06/03/eu-says-tech-sovereignty-package-about-protecting-citizens/), [NOS Nieuws](https://nos.nl/l/2616985)

**UK Competition Watchdog Forces Google to Give Publishers Control Over AI Search**
The UK's Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) ordered Google to give publishers control over how their content is used in AI overviews and when "fine-tuning" its AI models. This regulatory action has EU-wide precedent value for AI policy implementation and content governance, signaling that European regulators are willing to impose operational constraints on US AI firms' data practices.
