# Executive Summary

The European Commission's publication of a voluntary Code of Practice on AI-generated content marking represents a critical regulatory clarification for the Dutch startup ecosystem ahead of the AI Act's enforcement date of 2 August 2026. The Code operationalizes transparency obligations that will become legally binding within two months, creating immediate compliance requirements for generative AI providers and deployers—a category encompassing a substantial portion of Netherlands-based AI ventures. The Commission's accompanying info session (22 June 2026) signals active support for industry adoption, though the voluntary nature of the Code may create competitive disadvantages for early signatories relative to late-moving competitors, particularly given the tight timeline to mandatory compliance. This regulatory clarity should reduce legal uncertainty for Dutch AI startups, though compliance costs and labelling implementation challenges may disproportionately affect earlier-stage ventures with limited compliance infrastructure. The convergence of voluntary industry standards with imminent hard legal requirements presents both a market-structuring opportunity and a potential barrier to entry for smaller players in the generative AI space.
